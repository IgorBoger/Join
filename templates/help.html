Join Help Page

Your guide to using our Kanban project management tool. Here, we provide an overview of the functions of Join and how you can uplift your workflow to the next level!

What is Join?

Join is a Kanban-based project management tool designed and built by a group of dedicated students as part of their web development bootcamp at the Developer Akademie.


Kanban, a Japanese term meaning “billboard,” is a highly effective method to visualize work-in-progress and maximize efficiency. Join leverages Kanban principles to help users manage tasks and projects in an intuitive, visual interface.


Note: Join is designed as an educational exercise and is not intended for extensive business usage. While we strive for the best user experience, we cannot guarantee consistent availability, reliability, or accuracy.

How to Use It

1.
Exploring the Board
When you log in, you'll see a default board with four lists: To Do, In Progress, Await Feedback, Done.
2.
Creating Contacts
Add contacts in the Contacts-section by clicking “New Contact” and filling in the details.
3.
Adding Cards
Click the “+” button under a list to create a task. You can include task details like: Title, Description, Due date, and Assignees.
4.
Moving Cards
Drag and drop tasks between lists to track progress.
5.
Editing & Deleting Tasks
Click a card to modify its details or delete it using the Delete-button.
6.
Filtering & Searching
Use the search bar to find tasks by: Assignee, Due date, or Status.
7.
Customizing Your Board
Rename lists, add new columns, or change the order of existing ones.
8.
Notifications & Task Reminders
Get notified about updates and assigned tasks.

Troubleshooting & FAQ

  • Q: I can't log in. What should I do?
    A: Ensure your internet connection is stable. If the issue persists, try resetting your password.


  • Q: How can I invite teammates?
    A: Go to the Contacts-section, click “New Contact,” and enter their details.


  • Q: Can I recover deleted tasks?
    A: No, deleted tasks are permanently removed, so be careful when deleting.
